Bianca Champenois

Hello! My name is Bianca, and I am a senior at UC Berkeley in the Department of Mechanical Engineering.

In my free time, I like to fix bikes, play ultimate frisbee, read books, and run around Berkeley to find the best place to watch the sunset. I also enjoy darkroom photography and making things. Recently, I have been learning how to tie knots, pick locks, and win minesweeper as fast as I can.

On a broad level, I care about sustainability, energy, and transportation. On a more specific level, I am interested in dynamics, controls, and using computer science and math to solve relevant engineering problems. Above all, I am passionate about education.

At Berkeley, I work in the Environmental Fluid Mechanics and Hydrology lab where I run experiments to quantify methane emissions from surface flow in wetlands. During my summers, I have done research at the Tecnológico de Monterrey in Mexico on power converters for renewable energy integration and at the Technische Universität Hamburg in Germany on the control of nonlinear systems that can harvest ocean wave energy.
